# Funnels

Funnels in Actioneer track user progression through a defined sequence of events — from first touch to conversion, or any multi-step flow your product involves. They surface where users drop off, how long each step takes, and how different Segments compare.

---

## How Funnels work

A Funnel is a sequence of up to 10 steps, each defined by a user event. Actioneer evaluates your warehouse data to determine how many users completed each step and where they fell off.

| Component | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Steps** | Ordered sequence of events, e.g. App Open → Sign Up → KYC → First Investment |
| **Conversion rate** | Percentage of users who progressed from one step to the next |
| **Drop-off** | Users who completed step N but did not reach step N+1 |
| **Time between steps** | Median, mean, and P95 duration between consecutive steps |

---

## Creating a Funnel

Funnels can be created from Chat or from the Funnels page:

- **From Chat** — Ask "Show me the funnel from app download to first investment" and Actioneer builds it.
- **From the Funnels page** — Define steps manually by selecting events from your schema.

Each step can include property filters to narrow the scope — e.g. "Sign Up" where platform is Android, or "Purchase" where amount exceeds ₹500.

---

## Funnel analysis

Once a Funnel is defined, Actioneer provides several views:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Step-by-step conversion">
    See the raw count and percentage at each step. Identify the single biggest drop-off point in the flow.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Time-to-convert">
    Understand how long it takes users to move between steps. Median and P95 times help distinguish healthy progression from stalled users.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Segment comparison">
    Overlay two or more Segments on the same Funnel. Compare conversion rates between Android vs iOS users, paid vs organic, or any other dimension.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Drop-off diagnosis">
    For users who dropped at a specific step, see what events they performed after the drop — across sessions. This helps identify whether they abandoned entirely or took an alternative path.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

---

## Acting on Funnel insights

Funnels are not just for observation. When you spot a problem, you can act on it immediately:

- **Save drop-off users as a Segment** — Target the users who fell off at a specific step for re-engagement.
- **Pin to a Board** — Add the Funnel chart to a Board for ongoing monitoring.
- **Set an alert** — Monitor a specific step's conversion rate and get notified when it drops below a threshold.
- **Build a Campaign** — Push the drop-off Segment to your CLM and trigger a nudge.

<Info>
  Funnels support up to 10 steps. For longer flows, break them into sub-funnels — e.g. "Onboarding" and "First Transaction" as separate Funnels — and analyse them independently.
</Info>
