# How It Works

Actioneer sits as a single intelligence layer across your data warehouse, BI tools, and customer engagement platforms. It does not replace any of them. It connects to what you already have and adds three capabilities that no single tool in the stack provides: natural-language querying, persistent business memory, and an action layer.

## The three layers

### Query layer

Actioneer translates plain-English questions into SQL queries executed against your connected data warehouse. The translation is schema-aware — it reads your tables, columns, and relationships, so queries are grounded in your actual data model, not a generic guess.

When a user asks a question, Actioneer:
1. Parses the intent and identifies the data scope.
2. Maps the question to the relevant tables and metric definitions.
3. Generates and validates SQL before execution.
4. Returns the answer with the underlying query visible and auditable.

<Tip>
  Every answer includes the SQL that produced it. Nothing is a black box.
</Tip>

### Memory layer

Most AI tools start from scratch every conversation. Actioneer does not. It maintains a persistent knowledge base specific to your organisation:

- **Metric definitions** — what "conversion" or "retention" means at your company, not what the model assumes.
- **KPI targets** — the numbers your team tracks against.
- **Past campaigns** — what was run, when, and what moved.
- **Playbooks** — validated analytical workflows that can be reused and scheduled.
- **Team preferences** — how results should be formatted, which breakdowns matter.

This context compounds over time. The more Actioneer is used, the more precisely it understands your business.

### Action layer

Insights are only useful if they lead to action. Actioneer closes the gap between seeing a problem and doing something about it:

- **Push segments** to CleverTap, WebEngage, MoEngage, or other CLM tools in one click.
- **Build campaigns** directly from cohort analysis results.
- **Set up alerts** so that anomalies trigger Slack messages with root-cause context.
- **Schedule playbooks** to run on a cadence — daily, weekly, or every few hours.

## What happens when you ask a question

<Steps>
  <Step title="Intent parsed">
    Actioneer interprets the question, identifies the data scope, and determines which tables and metrics are relevant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plan generated">
    A query plan is built — potentially spanning multiple SQL queries, intermediate analysis steps, and validation checks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Queries executed">
    SQL is run against your connected warehouse. Results are validated against your metric definitions and business context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Answer delivered">
    The result is returned as a chart, table, or metric — along with the SQL used, so the answer is fully traceable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Action available">
    From the same result, you can save to a Board, build a segment, push a campaign, or pin the finding for later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connecting to your stack

Actioneer connects to over 700 data sources. No pipeline rebuild. No data migration. No schema changes required.

Setup involves three steps:
1. **Authenticate** your data warehouse (OAuth, service account, or access key — depending on the platform).
2. **Actioneer maps your schema** — tables, columns, relationships, and event taxonomy are discovered automatically.
3. **Your team starts asking questions** — metric definitions and playbooks build over time as the platform is used.

<Info>
  Actioneer operates as a read-only integration by default. It queries your data but does not create, modify, or delete anything in your warehouse or connected tools.
</Info>
