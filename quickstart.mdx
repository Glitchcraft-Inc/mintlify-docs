This quickstart takes you from zero to a working, read-only connection. You'll sign in to your workspace, connect one data source, and confirm Actioneer can see your tables — without granting any write access.

| At a glance | |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Goal** | One verified, read-only data connection |
| **Prerequisites** | An Actioneer workspace · admin access to one data source · ability to allowlist an IP |
| **Estimated time** | ~20 minutes |
| **Actioneer IP** | `35.244.14.238` (egress, for allowlisting) |

<Info>
  Not sure whether to connect a warehouse or stream events instead? See [Ingestion vs. warehouse connections](/concepts/ingestion-vs-warehouse) before you start.
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to your workspace">
    Your Actioneer workspace is provisioned during onboarding. Sign in with the credentials your team received. If you don't have access yet, ask your workspace admin or [reach out to us](/support).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your first data source">
    Pick the guide that matches your stack and follow it end to end. Each one creates a dedicated, least-privilege service identity and — where the source supports it — locks access to Actioneer's egress IP, `35.244.14.238`.

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Snowflake" icon="snowflake" href="/integrations/data-warehouses/snowflake">
        Create a read-only service user with a Programmatic Access Token.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Google BigQuery" icon="database" href="/integrations/data-warehouses/google-big-query">
        Grant least-privilege access via a GCP service account.
      </Card>
      <Card title="All data sources" icon="layers" href="/integrations/data-warehouses">
        Browse every warehouse, lake, and file source.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Stream events instead" icon="send" href="/ingestion">
        Push events directly with the ingestion API.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm the connection">
    Back in Actioneer, open **Data** in the left sidebar and select the source you just connected. A healthy connection lists the databases, schemas, and tables Actioneer can read.

    <Check>
      Your tables and views appear, and the connection is marked read-only. Actioneer holds only `SELECT`/`USAGE`-equivalent grants — it cannot create, modify, or delete anything in the source.
    </Check>

    <Tip>
      Point your first connection at a non-production schema if you'd like to validate access before exposing production data.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
